## Track M — Mesh generation pipeline (parallel to solver track)

Deliverable: `meshgen/` — scripted Gmsh generation of wake-embedded (M0/M1)
and wake-free (M3/M4, for Track B) tet meshes, coarse/medium/fine families per
case.

**Track-wide convention.** Model the wake as a Gmsh *embedded surface in volume*
(`gmsh.model.mesh.embed`); node duplication is NOT done in Gmsh — it is done by
the solver-side preprocessor (`mesh/wake_cut.py`), keeping meshing vanilla
(hard rule 8). The wake-**free** families (M3/M4) drop the embed step entirely:
the wake sheet survives only as the source of a size field, so nothing in the
mesh topology knows the wake exists — exactly the form Track B's level set
consumes (dual-mesh rule, Track B working rules). Any wake-cut change reruns the
topology asserts on all meshes in `cases/meshes/` (hard rule 7).

### M0 — Single-layer extruded NACA0012 quasi-2D mesh ✓ (closed 2026-07-06, with P2)
**Deliverable:** **Single-layer extruded NACA0012 quasi-2D mesh.** Input: 2D triangulated airfoil mesh (Gmsh). Extrude exactly ONE cell layer in z between two parallel planes; both z-planes tagged `symmetry`, other surfaces tagged `wall`, `farfield`, `wake`. Prisms subdivided into 3 tets each with a **globally consistent rule**: on every shared quad face, the diagonal connects to the smallest global node index; preprocessor assert that every interior quad face is split identically from both sides (no hanging nodes / slivers). **Planar wake surface embedded** from TE to far field (`gmsh.model.mesh.embed`; node duplication stays solver-side, unchanged). With a single layer there is exactly one spanwise station, so Γ is a single scalar — the simplest possible topology for the P2 master–slave elimination and Kutta loop. Coarse/medium/fine family, revised single-layer targets ~15k / 60k / 240k tets (≈ 5k/20k/80k 2D triangles × 3).
**Gate:**
- [x] Solver preprocessor ingests it; topology asserts pass (see P2); quad-split consistency assert passes; mesh family generated by one script, one parameter (h). Gate G2.5 (quasi-2D spanwise-zero) is the acceptance link between Track M and Track P.
**Visual test examples:**
- VM0.1 render mesh tags with a headless script (PyVista or ParaView batch) and verify `wake` is one continuous internal sheet from TE to far field, `symmetry` planes are planar and non-overlapping with `wall`.
- VM0.2 show element size (or edge-length histogram) for coarse/medium/fine on one figure; verify monotone refinement and similar growth ratio away from airfoil/wake.
**Notes:** doubles as the low-cost rapid test environment for P2–P4 and the
simplest `wake_cut.py` testbed. Delivery record: progress ledger, Track M.

### M2 — Wing-body: wake–fuselage junction ✓ CLOSED 2026-07-17 (mesh delivered 2026-07-13, user-directed; **body re-spec'd + regenerated 2026-07-16**; **solver leg closed by Track B / B9 2026-07-17**)
**Deliverable:** **ONERA M6 wing + simplified axisymmetric fuselage, fused into
a wing-body half model, meshed WAKE-FREE for the level-set (Track B) path.**
The 2026-07-10 note said the wake–fuselage intersection is the case the
conforming-sheet approach handles worst, and that M2 should be scheduled with
Track B's embedded wake, which removes the need to pre-embed a sheet. That is
exactly what this does: **nothing is fragmented or embedded** — the wake exists
only as a corridor size field, and the solver builds it from the analytic TE
polyline via `WakeLevelSet`. The mesh has **no `wake` group**.

`meshgen/fuselage.py` (`FuselageParams`, `radius_at`, `add_fuselage_solid`) +
`meshgen/wingbody.py` (`onera_m6_wingbody_mesh`, `te_polyline`, `junction_z`).
`wing3d.py` is **byte-untouched** — the wing loft, the M5 round cap and
`_collect_3d` are imported and reused, so every existing M1/M4/M5 family stays
bit-identical.

★ **The fuselage is ONE splined body of revolution, not four fused primitives.**
Fusing nose-ellipsoid + cylinder + cone + tail-sphere leaves a C0 seam at every
junction (the cylinder→cone shoulder especially), and a surface crease is a
spurious edge feature — the flat-tip-cap problem M5 exists to remove. A single
meridian profile splined through the same piecewise radius law and revolved
about the chord axis gives a C2 skin, so the only sharp wall features on the
whole wing-body are the wing LE/TE, which carry the physics.

★ **`junction_z = r_f` is EXACT, not approximate:** the M6 root chord
(x ∈ [0, 0.806]) lies wholly inside the constant-radius section, and the wing TE
sits at y = 0 (the ONERA D section has zero TE thickness), so the TE line meets
the fuselage skin at z = r_f exactly. That vertex is what the level-set wake's
inboard end attaches to.

★★ **BODY RE-SPEC 2026-07-16 (user-directed) — the 2026-07-13 body was not a
defensible subsonic wing-body.** It was 2.13 long with its nose tip **0.30
(= 0.37 C_ROOT) ahead of the wing root LE**, so the nose's own displacement
flow sat on the wing — at M∞ 0.5 that contaminates exactly the surface
pressures B9 exists to read. The body is now fixed by **rules, not station
numbers** (`fuselage.py`: `LENGTH_IN_ROOT_CHORDS`, `NOSE_DIAMETERS`,
`x_center`), and `FuselageParams` is parameterized by `length` + `x_center`
instead of `x_body_end`:
- **length = 5 C_ROOT = 4.031** (was 2.13), with the **wing root chord centered
  on the body**: 2 C_ROOT of body fore of the root LE and 2 C_ROOT aft of the
  root TE (measured **1.6122 / 1.6122**, `<level>_stats.csv`).
- **nose = half ellipsoid, x semi-axis = 2 body diameters = 0.60** (was 0.30 =
  1 diameter, a near-hemisphere) ⇒ fineness 4:1.
- **the body skin is no longer h_wall everywhere**: it relaxes to
  **h_body = 2 h_wall** away from the wing (the flow there is a smooth
  displacement field), stays at h_wall under the wing because the wing's own
  distance field wins the `Min`, and is **driven by the local body RADIUS at
  the two tips** (`wingbody._fuselage_field`).
- ★ **Why the tips need a radius law, measured not assumed:** a body of
  revolution's faceting angle is **h / R(x)**, and R collapses at both ends
  (r_tail = 30 mm; the sharper nose's tip curvature radius is now
  r_f²/l_nose = **37 mm**, half the old body's 75 mm). The first cut of this
  re-spec — constant h_body — put **20 of the 26 worst crease edges on the aft
  cone** (cone p99 **45.9°** vs cylinder 20.7°) and **failed the crease gate at
  29.4°**: h_body had faceted the afterbody into a polygonal needle. Ramping
  h_body_tip → h_body over the afterbody length reproduces h ≈ 0.4 R(x) along
  the cone almost exactly (a cone's radius grows linearly with distance from
  its tip) and fixed it: **cone p99 45.9 → 19.2°, overall p99 29.4 → 20.7°,
  max 49.7 → 24.9°**. Composed with **`Max`, not `Min`** — the tip balls' VOut
  is h_body, which under `Min` would cap the whole domain, far field included.
- **The wing is untouched:** h_wall/h_edge/h_tip and the loft are unchanged, so
  the **TE node counts are bit-identical** (76 coarse / 150 medium, before and
  after) and the Kutta stations B9 will build do not move. The cut-element
  counts legitimately did move (1,415 → **1,687** coarse; 29,108 → **28,360**
  medium): those live in the volume the body reshaped.
★★ **FAR FIELD ENLARGED, same day (user-directed follow-up).** The first cut of
the re-spec left `r_far = 15 MAC = 9.69` — the wing-ALONE convention inherited
from `wing3d` — and recorded the consequence as an open worry: the 1.9×-longer
body then spanned ~42% of the far-field diameter with only ~1.9 body lengths of
clearance off the tips. Once the body is the big object, the wing's MAC is the
wrong scale to size the domain by. Now `R_FAR = 25 MAC = 16.15`: **3.51 body
lengths clear of each tip** (measured 14.136 both ends) and the body down to
**12.5% of the far-field diameter** (both in `<level>_stats.csv`).
- ★ **h_far scales WITH r_far** (`H_FAR_IN_H_WALL` 120 → 200 = 120 × 25/15), and
  every FIXED refinement distance scales with h_far (`H_FAR_REF`, `grad`). This
  is the whole trick and it is not cosmetic: each Distance+Threshold climbs from
  its SizeMin to h_far across `0.55 r_far`, so holding **h_far / r_far** fixed
  holds every gradient — and therefore the mesh near the aircraft — exactly
  where it was, while the domain grows 2.78× in volume for **~nothing** (the
  added shell's cells grow with it). Left un-scaled, the fixed distances alone
  steepened the wake corridor enough to drop min dihedral to **0.17°**, under
  the 2.0° bound.
- ★ **The wake sheet now stops SHORT of the sphere** (`x_down = xc + 0.8 r_far`,
  was `xc + r_far + 0.5` — i.e. it used to *pierce* the boundary). The sheet is
  a size-field source only, so its extent is free, and dragging its h_wake
  corridor onto the OUTFLOW boundary put 0.087 m triangles on a sphere sized
  6.0 and produced **every bad tet in the mesh** (all 13 at the downstream pole,
  min dihedral 3.03°). Nothing is served by resolving a wake as it leaves a
  Dirichlet boundary.
- ★★ **`Mesh.OptimizeNetgen = 1`, and the finding that forced it.** This family
  has one structural fragility: the wake sheet's inboard edge sits at
  `z = z_junc = 0.15`, **hanging over open fluid everywhere aft of the body**,
  so its corridor prints a ~0.2 m-wide FINE RIBBON down the symmetry plane at
  y ≈ 0 against a plane at h_far, and the mesher answers with pancakes. Min
  dihedral on medium at 25 MAC by h_far/h_wall: **120 → 0.31°, 160 → 4.80°,
  200 → 2.63° — NOT monotone**. ⇒ it is a **LOTTERY**, and the pre-2026-07-16
  family (5.88°) was simply winning it; the quality bound was being defended by
  luck, not design. Netgen removes the ribbon's slivers outright: **0 tets under
  5° at every h_far tried**, min dihedral **11.0–13.6**. It touches only the
  volume — crease p99 (10.07) and every `_wingbody_asserts` boundary invariant
  are unchanged. **NOT set in `wing3d.py`**, so M1/M4/M5 stay bit-identical.
- **Net:** a 2.78×-larger domain, **BETTER quality than the family ever had**
  (coarse dihedral 5.60 → **19.49**, aspect 11.6 → **5.5**; medium 5.88 →
  **11.04**, aspect 6.35 → **4.83**) on **FEWER tets** (71,393 → **65,621**;
  583,470 → **533,129**).
- **Recorded, still true:** r_far is now sized off the BODY, so the wing-body no
  longer shares the wing-alone families' 15-MAC far field. The A/B against them
  at equal `h_wall` is still controlled where it matters (the wing skin sizing
  and its triangulation are untouched — TE nodes bit-identical), but the
  wing-body now carries LESS far-field error than the wing-alone comparison,
  which is the honest direction for a wing-body but is a difference to quote if
  a cl is ever compared across the two families.

### M5 — Rounded ONERA M6 tip cap ✓ (NEW 2026-07-13, user-directed; closed 2026-07-13)
**Deliverable:** **The M6 tip cap, rounded — the geometry fix P13/G13.3 asked
for.** M1 closes the wing with a **flat** tip cap: a documented deliberate
simplification, standard for FP validation meshes, and the third and last of the
defects that blocked a three-point Richardson on ONERA M6. A flat cap meets the
upper/lower surfaces at a **sharp convex edge**, and in potential flow a sharp
convex edge is a singularity — G13.3's box study measured its peak Mach
**diverging** under uniform refinement (p = +0.321) while the wake free edge
(+0.045, fixed by G13.2) and the wing interior (−0.014) stayed bounded. It is
**not a P11 problem**: isoparametric *elements* cannot regularize a genuinely
sharp geometric *edge*. The geometry is what is wrong.

`meshgen/wing3d.py` gains **`tip_cap="round"`** (default `"flat"` ⇒ every
existing family bit-identical, so the P5 / P8-G8.2 / B7 / M1 locks are
untouched): the wing is closed by the **half body of revolution swept by the tip
section about its own chord line**, i.e. `{√(y² + (z−B_SEMI)²) ≤ t(x)}` with
`t(x)` the tip section's local half thickness. Built as an OCC revolve of the
tip section's upper half-face about an edge OF that face (the degenerate-at-the-
axis kind, like a sphere from a half disc), fused onto the loft.

★ **Why this construction and not another:** the cap radius **vanishes at the LE
and the TE**, so the cap degenerates to a *point* at each — which means the **TE
line, the wake sheet, the tip TE corner, the Kutta stations and B_SEMI are all
UNCHANGED**. Only the tip WALL moves. That is what makes the A/B against M1
controlled, and it is why no solver-side plumbing was needed. (Verified
analytically and asserted at generation: the revolved solid protrudes past the
wing by at most **4e-6 m** — three orders below the finest edge size — and never
reaches aft of the local TE, so it can neither cut the wake sheet nor spawn
slivers.)
